The Moon Is Down

John Steinbeck — 1942

In high school, I read "East of Eden" and so I was curious about this Steinbeck novella I found in a free library while walking around the Outer Sunset, SF. In this novella, Steinbeck tells a fictional story of a military occupation in a small town in Europe.

The story is a powerful exploration of human resilience and the spirit of resistance amongst the townfolk. This is contrasted with the occupying army who are portrayed as cold, calculated, and controlled by their orders. As the story progresses, we see the psychological toll of the occupation on both sides.

Heart of Darkness

Joseph Conrad — 1899

"Heart of Darkness" is a novella by Joseph Conrad, published in 1899. The story follows Charles Marlow as he journeys up the Congo River in search of a man named Kurtz, who is rumored to be a brilliant and powerful ivory trader.

The novella is a critique of European colonialism and the brutal treatment of indigenous peoples. It explores themes of imperialism, racism, and the darkness of the human soul. I was particularly moved by the prose and psychological depth of the narrator.

Things Fall Apart

Chinua Achebe — 1958

"Things Fall Apart" is a novel by Chinua Achebe. The story follows Okonkwo, a warrior from an Ibo village in Nigeria. In the first part of the story, Okonkwo navigates the challenges of becoming a respected man despite his father's failures. In the latter part of the story, he faces the cultural upheaval and social conflict brought by British colonialism and Christianity to his community.

I first read "Things Fall Apart" in high school literature class. After seeing it sit on my family's bookshelf for a while, I decided to reread it. In high school I remember drawing connections between Okonkwo & Oedipus, two strong characters with similar tragic flaws.
